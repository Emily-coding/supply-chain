Methodology: Leontief price-side IRF from ONS Supply and Use Tables

Data source. ONS Input-Output Analytical Tables, Industry by Industry, reference year 2023 (Blue Book 2025 vintage). 101 non-energy industries × 101 industries. The A coefficient matrix (direct input requirements per £1 of output) is taken directly from ONS sheet "A"; the long-run Leontief inverse L = (I−A)⁻¹ from sheet "Leontief".

Energy partition. Three industries are treated as exogenous price-setters whose output prices are shocked: C19 (Coke & refined petroleum), D351 (Electricity generation, transmission & distribution), D352_3 (Gas manufacture & distribution). All other 101 industries are endogenous.

Long-run price model. Partition A into energy (E) and rest (R) blocks. With no change in primary-input costs elsewhere, the steady-state response of non-energy producer prices to an energy shock ΔpE is:

ΔpR = (I − ARR′)⁻¹ · AER′ · ΔpE

This is the full pass-through ceiling — every firm instantly and fully passes costs on.

Dynamic IRF (Neumann series). Real-world price adjustment is staggered. The round-by-round expansion, where each round ≈ one quarter, is:

ΔpR(1) = AER′ ΔpE  (direct impact)
ΔpR(k) = AER′ ΔpE + ARR′ ΔpR(k−1)  (adds one supply-chain step per quarter)
limk→∞ ΔpR(k) = (I − ARR′)⁻¹ AER′ ΔpE

Pass-through regimes. "Full" applies ρ = 1 everywhere. "Realistic" applies sector-specific ρⱼ ∈ [0.35, 1.0] as a round-level dampener, calibrated to trade exposure and market power. Commodity, trade-exposed EII sectors (steel 0.35, non-ferrous metals 0.35, industrial gases 0.40, glass/ceramics 0.50, paper 0.55) reflect the Ganapati et al. incomplete pass-through result (~70% avg US manufacturing, lower for competitive industries). Food, utilities and services (ρ = 0.90–1.00) reflect LMM's near-complete pass-through for domestically-oriented concentrated sectors. The gap between full and realistic at q12 is the portion absorbed via margin compression or output reduction.

Shock scenarios. Unit: +10% to each energy product price simultaneously. 2022-calibrated: refined petroleum +50%, electricity +200%, gas +250% — broadly matching peak wholesale moves from 2021 Q1 to 2022 Q3. Results scale linearly with shock size.

Adjustment margin classification. Derived from ρⱼ: ρ ≤ 0.55 → Quantity-led (large residual absorbed in output/margins); 0.55 < ρ < 0.85 → Mixed; ρ ≥ 0.85 → Price-led. Corroborated by ONS (2025): energy-intensive manufacturing output −33.6% from 2021 Q1 to 2024 Q4.

Source: ONS Input-Output Analytical Tables, Industry × Industry, 2023 reference year (Blue Book 2025). Pass-through calibration: Ganapati/Shapiro/Walker (AEJ Applied 2020); Lafrogne-Joussier/Martin/Méjean (CEPII 2023). See uk-energy-exposure.md for full reference list.
