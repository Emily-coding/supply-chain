Who drives CPI 04.5 (electricity, gas, fuels)?

Contribution = household-expenditure share in the division × industry PPI move. D351 (electricity) and D352_3 (gas) account for essentially all of it.

Who drives CPI 01 (food)?

Food-manufacturing heterogeneity: agriculture (A01) and dairy (C105) dominate; the ~3pp gap between observed (33.3%) and predicted (30.2%) is a good fit for a goods-only bridge.

Industries by direct energy cost share

Ranked by direct gas + electricity + oil share of output from the 2023 IxI A matrix. ρpeak = observed peak PPI move ÷ Leontief-predicted move from avg-2022 energy shock. ρ below 1 ⇒ partial pass-through or volume-loss margin; ρ above 1 ⇒ excess pass-through or other cost shocks.

Caveats

  • SPPI coverage is incomplete. 27 services industries are in via ONS SPPI (quarterly, held flat across months). Still missing: owner-occupiers' housing (imputed rent — dominates CPI 04 housing weight), health, education retail, R&D, travel agencies, cultural/recreational services, public admin, construction output. CPI division 04 is still over-predicted for this reason — the sub-divisions 04.3/04.4/04.5 themselves are fine.
  • Retail / transport margins ignored. The bridge treats factory-gate PPI as the consumer-facing price. Any margin change between 2021 and 2024 shows up as "gap" rather than being modelled.
  • Within-bucket 4-digit CPA weights are not available. Where an IOAT bucket contains multiple 4-digit CPA PPI series and ONS does not publish a bucket-level aggregate, we use simple mean across components. 28/51 buckets use the ONS aggregate directly; 20 fall back to simple mean.
  • N=1 shock. The 2022 episode is a single correlated gas/oil/electricity event; ρ estimates are conditional on that shock, not a structural parameter. Co-moving sterling depreciation and post-Covid demand are confounders.
  • Internal shock definition. The Leontief "shock" is currently the UK PPI move for D351/D352_3/C19, which already bakes in retail/regulatory pass-through. Switching to external wholesale (DESNZ NBP gas, N2EX baseload electricity, BoE Brent sterling) would separate upstream from downstream pass-through.
  • CPA→COICOP proportions from 2019. Latest pre-Covid year; basket shifts since then are not captured in the weights.
